# MVP — lean PRD for the minimum *sellable* version

Idea: whatever the user passed when invoking this skill.

Respond in the user's language. Goal: the smallest thing someone will pay for, shippable in roughly two weeks. Ruthlessly cut. A feature that isn't required to charge money is out of scope for v1.

Produce a PRD with these sections:

1. **Value proposition** — one sentence.
2. **Target user** — specific, not "businesses". The exact person who pays.
3. **Core job-to-be-done** — the one outcome they hire this for.
4. **The single core flow** — the one path the product must do well, described step by step.
5. **Out of scope (v1)** — explicitly list what you are NOT building yet. Be aggressive.
6. **Screens** — minimal screen/route list, just enough to start designing.
7. **Data model** — the few entities and fields actually needed.
8. **Success metric** — one metric that proves it works, and how you measure it.
9. **Monetization** — price, model, and exactly when you charge (ideally before or at first value).
10. **First 10 paying users** — a concrete plan. No "do marketing"; name the channel and the action.
11. **Build plan** — milestones to a sellable version with a rough time estimate. Flag the riskiest piece to build first.
12. **Validate-before-building** — anything to confirm with a real user before writing more code.

If the idea is too big to ship in two weeks, say so and propose the cut-down version.
